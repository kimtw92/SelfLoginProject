Nurture core values to fulfill a city's vision and create high performance of education

Consolidation of policy education to realize national vision and Incheon city’s policies

  • Practice and experience-centered training to create future values including low carbon green growth
    • Low carbon, green growth, environment/green belt, history and culture courses
  • Economic education and regulations reform education to promote regional economic activation.
    • Reflect FTA, fair international trade, economy and regulations reform in the professional programs
  • Reflect major city policies in all the courses for elementary knowledge on general fields

Expansion of the reading course to help a vision of "Incheon Encouraging its Citizens to Read Books"

  • Expansion of the specialized reading course: 2 sessions in 2010 → 5 sessions or more in 2011
  • Form at least a two hour-subject on reading for the group course(5 days or more)
  • Grand a leave for reading books in the long-term program

Consolidation of education on security and public office value

  • Consolidation of education on public office value and the establishment of a mission and a view on ethics
    • Shape the special lecture, "Time to talk with the organization head," for the basic and long period education
    • Implement a course for the integrity education of public officials and cyber education for the integrity enhancement of all employees
  • Consolidate security education, according to recent security threats from North Korea
    • Open a course to cope with emergencies, and form relevant courses for the basic and professional programs
    • ※ Special lectures by outside instructors, work group discussion, and audio visual media use

Training for newly appointed public officials under the principle of "Training first, appointments later"

  • Expansion of training duration to consolidate the mindset of public officials on public office and core value education: 3 weeks → 4 weeks
  • Human nature education, civil complaints handling and telephone response, labor-management relations of public officials, service education, etc.

Strengthening Global Leadership to Lead Future Vision

Foreign language skills consolidation to foster global leaders.

  • Solving the demand on change in education, which has arisen from the adjustment of the prescribed number of trainees for the excellent foreign language courses
    • 50 trainees for learning English in 2010 → 35 trainees and 15 for learning Chinese in 2011
    • Execute the MBO(management by objectives) regarding foreign language levels: Improve scores by more than 30% before the trainees receive the foreign language courses
    • Implement 3 or more of the recognized tests to enhance the effects of education
    • Induce institutions to actively use the trainees for various events and international exchange work
  • Expansion of cyber foreign language education for public officials
    • English in 2010 (city, mandate) → English, Chinese, and Japanese(city, gun,gu, by application)
    • Conversation learning focused on speaking and listening

Enhancement of an organization's core capabilities through leadership capacity consolidation

  • Courses to nurture core middle managers
    • Intensively study subjects that are closely related with major city policies
    • ※ Top six areas: Local administration, commerce and logistics, urban planning, social welfare, transportation policy, and environmental policy
    • Develop capabilities to solve problems through work group study on pending issues
    • ※ Systematic study activity guidance from professional advisors by task
  • Recognition of role change of each position in line with the characteristics of positions and the strengthening of competitiveness
    • Newly open the leadership courses by position for public officials of grades 5, 6, 7, 8, and under

Create a Global mindset so as to successfully host the 2014 Asian games

  • Operate courses to consolidate competitiveness of Incheon as an international city
    • Newly open courses on international events planning/operation, international exchange cooperation, and how to properly carry out English presentation
  • Education on multicultural understanding and on the international level of global etiquettes
    • Newly open global culture understanding and multicultural society understanding courses

Consolidation of Practical and Professional Training to Improve Organizational Performance

Improving the efficiency of the group training through the coordination of work and learning

  • Operation of blended learning between group training and cyber education.
    • Pre-learning by cyber education before group training: 18 courses, including international exchange cooperation
  • Enhancement of learning effects and reducing the work burden through diversifying training durations
    • 5 days training(19 courses), 4 days training(2 courses), 3 days training(28 courses), 2 days training(2 courses), and 1 day training(1 course)

The expansion and bolstering of job-oriented professional training by field

  • Learning to actively cope with the demand for new social welfare and training
    • Multicultural society, human resources development, social welfare and administration courses
  • The expansion and segmentation of professional technical courses for balanced city establishment
    • Newly open courses on facility maintenance/repair, in-depth onsite management and supervision, and in-depth construction claims work
  • Consolidate job training to promote regional economic activation
    • Newly open the practical work for balanced city development, Incheon industry activation, and social corporate professional courses
  • Bolster participatory training, such as onsite-centered practices, study, and discussion
    • Problem-solving style of hands-on experience and participatory and discussion education and onsite learning

The enhancement of the training program's quality through continuously redesigning the training course

  • Re-design the courses by analyzing the training operation results and reflecting user opinions
    • Analyze the consistency of the courses with learning goals, and whether subjects are suitable, along with trainees’ satisfaction on instructors, etc.
    • Reflect the details of the questionnaire survey targeting trainees and the opinions of the personnel in charge of the course design and training operation

The expansion of the use of inside faculty and the securing of excellent instructors

  • Consolidating professional training by using high ranking public officials who are equipped with ample administrative experiences and expertise
    • Bolster job capabilities of the trainees by using public officials in the related fields as faculty members
  • Manage the pool of excellent instructors by field
    • Secure excellent instructors through private training institutions, and municipal, provincial, and local colleges

Establishment of a Frequent Learning System by Consolidating Cyber Learning

The expansion and diversification of e-Learning for the frequent learning system

  • Expand the various cyber education opportunities by job field.
    • 240 sessions for 30 courses in 2010 → 360 sessions for 40 courses in 2011
  • Consolidating online learning selection rights and accessibility through offering various learning channels.
    • Offer various learning channels to the public officials including regular courses, open lectures, lectures via video, etc
  • E-learning contents renewal offering for the enhancement of the quality level of the courses offered
    • Revise and supplement e-job professional courses with up-to-date contents and subjects

Continuous operation of the Cyber Education Center for the e-Learning service for citizens

  • Increase the number of members and the number of trainees.
    • Number of members: 103,085 → 115,000 people. Number of trainees: 120,000 → 145,000 people.
  • Diverse courses and the expansion of subjects
    • 200 subjects in 5 fields (foreign languages, digitalization, certificates/licenses, liberal arts, and the practical public administration fields).
  • Actively expand courses on certificates/licenses to support employment.
    • 7 courses, including APT management certificate, information processing engineers, and general administrative grade 9 public officials
  • Effective learning support and thorough learning management through the Customer Service Center
    • Support learning through the PC remote support system
    • Answer the questions and inquiries generated in learning through the Q&A session
    • Send SMS and e-mails once a month to encourage learning

Support for the joint-use of the cyber education operating system

  • Support for the joint use of the educational operating system and its contents (institutions wishing to receive cyber education within the concerned region)
    • 14 institutions including Michuhol Library, Incheon Metro, Incheon Urban Development Corporation, etc
